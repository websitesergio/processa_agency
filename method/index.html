The Processa 14-Day Deployment Method

No discovery calls that go nowhere. No proposal decks. No hidden contingencies. A fixed 14-day engagement with a confirmed deliverable on every single day. This is the Processa method for deploying Answer Engine Optimisation and sub-60-second lead routing infrastructure into a dental practice. The engagement is a structured, day-by-day sequence of activities, each designed to build on the previous day's work. By day 14, your practice has a live, functioning patient acquisition infrastructure fully integrated with your existing systems. No migration period. No beta phase. No "it's fine once we work the bugs out." Day 14 is live day.

Days 1–5: Diagnostic Phase

The first five days are entirely diagnostic. There are no deployments, no integration work, and no infrastructure changes. Instead, the Processa team conducts a comprehensive audit of your practice's existing patient acquisition infrastructure, competitive positioning, and revenue opportunities. Day 1 covers onboarding, access provisioning, and establishment of communication protocols. Your practice manager, clinical lead, and patient coordinator are assigned as primary contacts. Access is provisioned to your website backend, analytics platforms, practice management system, and any existing ad account interfaces.

Days 2 and 3 cover technical AEO audit. The team evaluates your clinic's existing schema markup, analyses competitor AEO infrastructure across your region and treatment categories, maps the highest-intent patient queries your clinic receives or should receive, and benchmarks your current content architecture against competitor content. This audit identifies the specific keywords and patient intents where your practice is currently invisible to AI search systems and where your competitors are establishing citation authority.

Day 4 covers pipeline haemorrhage reconstruction. The team analyses your practice's enquiry data from the preceding 12 months, estimates the number of lost conversions caused by slow response time, calculates the revenue associated with those lost conversions, and constructs a financial baseline for the engagement. This calculation is data-driven and specific to your practice. It is not an estimate. Day 5 delivers the diagnostic brief to your entire practice leadership. The brief includes the audit findings, the revenue opportunity quantification, a competitive positioning assessment, and a confirmed scope for the remaining nine days of deployment.

Days 6–10: Architecture Phase

Days 6 through 10 involve the actual build-out of infrastructure. Day 6 begins schema deployment. The team builds and tests your clinic's full JSON-LD markup suite: Organisation schema (verified credentials and entity details), Service schema (for each treatment type), FAQ schema, and LocalBusiness schema if applicable. This markup is deployed to your website and tested for correctness using Google's Structured Data Testing Tool and competitor benchmarking.

Days 7 and 8 cover AEO content architecture. The team builds a content outline for your clinic's core treatment pages, ensuring each page comprehensively answers the highest-intent patient queries for that treatment. For an implant practice, this means building or refining your implant treatment page to thoroughly address cost, timeline, bone health requirements, and comparison to other tooth replacement options. For an Invisalign practice, this means building comprehensive treatment content specific to your practice and patient population. Content is optimised for both AI citation authority and human readability. The goal is pages that AI systems recognise as authoritative and that human patients find helpful and convincing.

Day 9 covers routing infrastructure deployment. The sub-60-second lead routing system is deployed, connected to your website enquiry forms, your practice management system, and your chosen communication channels (SMS, WhatsApp, email). Qualification rules are configured specific to your treatment types and patient screening criteria. Response templates are written, branded, and tested for each treatment type. API integrations are confirmed and tested end-to-end.

Day 10 is test day. The full system is tested: test enquiries are submitted through every active source (website form, Meta lead form, Google Ads landing page, etc.), and response accuracy and latency are verified. The routing system is confirmed to respond within 60 seconds. Your patient coordinator receives and acknowledges the test follow-up tasks. Attribution dashboard access is provisioned and verified. By end-of-day 10, all infrastructure is ready for live activation.

Days 11–14: Activation Phase

Day 11 is go-live day for the sub-60-second lead routing system. Real patient enquiries are now flowing through the automated routing system. The Processa team monitors response accuracy and latency continuously throughout day 11 to 13. Your patient coordinator is briefed on new follow-up task workflows and confirms understanding of the routing context data provided with each lead. Day 11 evening includes a full system handover call with your practice team, confirming that all staff are trained on the new routing workflows.

Day 12 covers your clinic's first Answer Engine Optimisation targets. The team identifies the top three to five high-intent patient queries for your practice and confirms your clinic's current visibility (citation rate) in AI search results for those queries. Day 12 also includes deployment of your content architecture updates to live and confirmation that schema markup is correctly indexed by Google and Bing.

Day 13 is dashboard handover day. You receive full access to the Processa Attribution and Patient Acquisition Centre (PAC) dashboard, which provides real-time visibility into: all inbound enquiries (source, treatment type, timing), routing response metrics (SLA compliance, response channel, coordinator acknowledgement time), and conversion tracking (enquiry to consultation booked, consultation to treatment started). The PAC dashboard is your ongoing command centre for monitoring acquisition performance.

Day 14 is confirmation day. The team confirms the sub-60-second routing SLA is maintained across all live enquiries, verifies your practice's AEO citation authority in the top three target queries, delivers the final PAC baseline report, and formally concludes the engagement. Your practice now owns all infrastructure. No ongoing retainer is required. The routing system, schema markup, content architecture, and attribution dashboard remain active indefinitely and require no service fees.

A Fixed Engagement, Not a Retainer

The Processa engagement is structured as a fixed 14-day project with a single deliverable timeline, not as an ongoing retainer. The engagement ends on day 14. The infrastructure you have built does not. Every element of the system — the lead routing automation, the schema markup, the content pages, the PAC dashboard — belongs to your practice. You own it entirely. You can modify it, integrate it with other systems, or migrate it to different infrastructure without permission or additional fees. You can manage it internally or engage other vendors to manage it on your behalf. The engagement is not dependent on an ongoing retainer or monthly service contract.

This structure reflects a fundamental belief: your patient acquisition infrastructure should be owned by your practice, not by your vendor. You should not be locked into a monthly retainer dependency where cancellation means loss of all infrastructure or customer data. Processa builds your infrastructure, transfers complete ownership to you, and then steps back. If you choose to engage for ongoing optimization, quarterly reporting, or additional deployment phases, those conversations happen after day 14 and are structured as separate engagements with separate timelines and deliverables.

Who This Is For

Processa deploys with Implant and Invisalign practices in the United Kingdom, Ireland, Spain, the Netherlands, and Germany. Ideal practices meet the following criteria: minimum five years in operation (this establishes clinical credibility and historical data for baseline calculations), minimum two new patient mandates per month in either Implant or Invisalign categories (this provides sufficient volume to achieve statistical significance from routing and AEO optimization), clinical leadership committed to participating in the diagnostic phase and confirming strategic direction, and willingness to integrate with the routing infrastructure without requiring extensive custom modification.

Practices meeting these criteria typically see measurable returns on the Processa engagement within the first 30 days of deployment. Lead routing improvements generate immediate conversion rate improvements. AEO citation authority compounds over 60 to 90 days and generates incremental organic enquiry volume. The combination produces a sustained, compounding improvement in patient acquisition efficiency across the engagement and beyond.
